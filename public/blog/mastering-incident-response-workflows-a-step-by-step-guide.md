## Understanding
The incident lifecycle is a structured framework that guides the process of handling system anomalies.
It includes several stages:

1. **Detection and Logging**: Incidents are identified through automated monitoring or user reports, and detailed logging ensures comprehensive tracking. **Categorization and Prioritization**: Incidents are categorized to determine their nature and urgency, ensuring they are addressed promptly by the appropriate teams. **Initial Diagnosis and Escalation**: The initial analysis aims to pinpoint the root cause, with escalation procedures in place if further expertise is needed.

**Resolution and Closure**: Corrective actions are taken to restore normal service, followed by formal closure and user notification. **Post-Incident Review**: After closure, reviews are conducted to analyze the incident and derive lessons for future prevention.
### Key Roles in Incident Management
Several roles are integral to effective incident response workflows:

- **Incident Manager**: Oversees the entire process, coordinating with teams to ensure timely resolution.
- **Service Desk Analysts**: Serve as the first point of contact, providing initial diagnostics and incident logging. - **IT Support Teams**: Provide specialized expertise for complex incidents beyond the service desk's capabilities. - **Users**: Play a crucial role in promptly reporting incidents and cooperating with IT teams.
