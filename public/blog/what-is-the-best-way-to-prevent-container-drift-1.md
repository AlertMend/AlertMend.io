## Understanding Container Drift in Cloud Environments

Container drift is the unintended deviation of container behavior from its original configuration. This can occur due to manual updates, unauthorized modifications, or unexpected configuration changes. Such drifts not only jeopardize security but also disrupt operational consistency. Identifying drift is essential for maintaining the integrity of containerized applications.

### Common Causes of Container Drift

- **Manual Configuration Changes**: Administrators manually altering settings or applying patches can lead to drift.
- **Unauthorized Software Updates**: New libraries or updates inadvertently introduced during runtime can modify container behavior.
- **Legacy Practices**: Continuing to use outdated approaches can inadvertently introduce drift, making systems vulnerable.

## Technical Insights: How Drift Detection Works

Preventing container drift is crucial for maintaining a secure and stable environment. Here are some of the best practices:

### Enforce Immutability

Embrace the principle of immutability by ensuring containers are not modified post-deployment. This approach limits potential attack vectors and maintains system consistency.
